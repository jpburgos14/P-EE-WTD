What is Light Pollution?

Light pollution refers to excessive artificial lighting that diminishes the visibility of stars and celestial bodies, disrupting ecosystems and human circadian rhythms. It's a growing problem in our modern world, with various sources contributing to the issue.

There are several types of light pollution, including:

  • Skyglow: The brightening of the night sky over populated areas, making it difficult to see stars.
  • Glare: Excessive brightness that causes visual discomfort and can impair vision.
  • Light Trespass: Unwanted or intrusive light spilling into unwanted areas, such as neighboring properties.
  • Clutter: Bright, confusing, and excessive groupings of light sources, often found in urban areas.

The main sources of light pollution include:

  • Urban areas: Streetlights, buildings, and advertising contribute significantly to light pollution.
  • Industrial facilities: Factories and other industrial sites often have bright lights that illuminate the night sky.
  • Outdoor lighting: Poorly designed or excessive outdoor lighting in residential areas also plays a role.
