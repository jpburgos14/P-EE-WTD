What Can You Do?

Reduce Your Light Footprint

You can make a significant impact by adopting simple practices:

  • Use energy-efficient lighting:** Opt for LED bulbs, which consume less energy and last longer.
  • Shield your lights:** Direct light downward to avoid unnecessary upward light pollution.
  • Turn off lights when not in use:** This simple habit can save energy and reduce light pollution.
  • Set timers:** Use timers to automate your outdoor lighting, ensuring it's only on when needed.
  • Support dark sky initiatives:** Join local organizations working to preserve dark skies and advocate for responsible lighting policies.

Spread the Word

Educate others about the importance of dark skies and the impact of light pollution:

  • Share information:** Discuss light pollution with friends, family, and colleagues.
  • Support organizations:** Donate to organizations working to protect dark skies.
  • Write to your representatives:** Advocate for policies that promote responsible lighting.

Support organizations like the International Dark-Sky Association (IDA).

Take Action

Get involved in local initiatives to reduce light pollution:

  • Participate in community events:** Join local stargazing events or volunteer for dark sky preservation projects.
  • Contact your local government:** Encourage your local government to adopt responsible lighting ordinances.
